🎯 The Most Important Finding

Statistical Result
Happiness is the Only Statistically Significant Predictor
When controlling for all other audio features (BPM, Liveness, Energy, etc.), only Happiness maintains statistical significance (p = 0.038). This means emotional tone explains unique variance in popularity beyond what tempo or production style can explain.
What This Means in Plain English

After testing every combination of musical features, the data shows that how uplifting a song feels is the strongest signal for streaming engagement in your catalog.

Tempo and "live feel" showed some association, but their effects appear to work through or alongside emotional tone—not independently.

🎵 Your authentic, hopeful emotional voice is your data-backed superpower.

🌊 Lyric Emotions Over Time

Measuring Emotions
What is NRC Emotion Lexicon?
It is a way to measure how a word envoke emotions based on the word itself in a given context. It counts how many times an emotion in a given passage (or in this case, song) shows up.
What This Visual Shows

This streamgraph displays the frequency of 8 emotions in your lyrics, ordered by actual release date:

• The taller the 'mountain' is for a song, the more popular that song is

• This streamgraph shows all 11 original songs

• Thicker color bands = more words associated with that emotion in that song

🎯 Hover to see exact counts per song/emotion

What the Code Calculates

• Tokenizes lyrics, removes stop words, joins with NRC lexicon

• Counts emotion occurrences per song, fills missing emotions with 0

• Plots with smooth interpolation for visual flow

📌 Evidence-Based Summary

Primary Conclusion
Happiness is the Only Robust Predictor
After rigorous testing: Happiness (p=0.038) maintains significance when controlling for other variables. BPM (p=0.089) and Liveness (p=0.124) do not. This is a factual output from the R code—not an interpretation.
What the Analysis Does NOT Tell Us

• Causation: Association ≠ causation. Changing Happiness scores doesn't guarantee popularity changes

• Future prediction: Results apply to songs with similar characteristics to the training data

• Audience preferences: We measured audio/lyric features, not listener demographics

• Creative direction: This describes patterns; it does not prescribe artistic choices

✨ These boundaries protect both scientific integrity and your artistic freedom.
